Navigating Your Inheritance with Confidence and Clarity

Receiving an inheritance is often a deeply emotional experience, tied to the loss of a loved one. Amidst the grief, you may be faced with the sudden responsibility of managing a significant amount of wealth. We specialize in providing financial advice for women who have received an inheritance — empowering you to make informed decisions that honor your loved one's legacy.

Inheritance Laws Across Southeast Asia

The legal landscape of inheritance in Southeast Asia is a complex tapestry of civil law, common law, and religious or customary laws. Understanding these laws is the first step to protecting your inheritance.

General Tax Implications

Even in countries without a direct inheritance tax, other taxes may apply:

  • •Capital Gains Tax: If you sell an inherited asset, you may be liable for tax on any increase in its value.
  • •Real Estate Transfer Tax: The transfer of property ownership may be subject to a specific transfer tax.
  • •Stamp Duty: May be levied on certain documents related to the transfer of assets.

Investing Your Inheritance

  • •Take Your Time: Avoid making major financial decisions in the immediate aftermath of a loved one's passing.
  • •Create a Financial Roadmap: A comprehensive plan based on your personal goals, risk tolerance, and values.
  • •Explore Your Options: From stocks and bonds to real estate and gender-lens investing.

Understanding Your Inheritance in Indonesia

Inheriting assets in Indonesia presents a unique set of challenges due to its pluralistic legal system. As a woman, it is vital to understand your rights to ensure you are empowered to manage your legacy effectively.

Legal Considerations

Indonesia's legal framework for inheritance is a complex mix of three systems:

  • •Civil Code (KUHPerdata): Applies to non-Muslims. Includes "forced heirship" guaranteeing a portion to spouse and children. Men and women have equal inheritance rights.
  • •Islamic Law: For Muslims, distribution follows specific shares from the Quran, where male heirs often receive double the share of female heirs of the same rank.
  • •Customary Law (Adat): Varying between ethnic groups, these laws can significantly impact inheritance.

Tax Implications

  • •Property Transfer Tax (BPHTB): When you inherit real estate, you are liable for this tax.
  • •Unpaid Taxes: As an heir, you become responsible for any of the deceased's unpaid taxes.
  • •Foreign Ownership: Foreigners cannot own freehold land. If you inherit such property, you have one year to sell or transfer it.

Investment & Planning

  • •Draft a Will: With a complex legal system, creating a will with a local notary is highly recommended.
  • •Consider Trusts: Increasingly common for managing complex assets, especially for foreigners.
  • •Get Legal Advice: Expert advice on structuring your holdings is critical if you are a foreigner.

Maximizing Your Inheritance in Singapore

Inheriting assets in Singapore provides a unique advantage due to its clear legal framework and tax-friendly policies. Our goal is to help you leverage this environment to protect and grow your wealth.

Legal Considerations

  • •Clear Laws: The Intestate Succession Act (non-Muslims) and AMLA (Muslims) provide clear hierarchy for estate distribution.
  • •Power of a Will: Ensures your assets are distributed precisely as you wish, appoint guardians, and name an executor.
  • •The Women's Charter: Guarantees women the right to own and manage property independently.

Tax Implications

  • •No Inheritance Tax: Singapore abolished estate duty in 2008. No capital gains tax or gift tax either.
  • •Income Tax on Estate: While the inheritance itself isn't taxed, any income generated by the estate after death is subject to income tax (e.g., rental income).

Investment & Planning

  • •Priority Banking: Place funds in a priority banking account for a dedicated relationship manager.
  • •Stocks & ETFs: Traditional options for long-term growth and diversification.
  • •Supplementary Retirement Scheme (SRS): Tax relief while investing for retirement.
  • •Trusts: Advanced asset protection and controlled inheritance distribution.
  • •Digital Assets: Include instructions in your will for cryptocurrency and online accounts.

Managing Your Inheritance in the Philippines

Receiving an inheritance in the Philippines involves navigating specific legal and tax obligations defined by the Civil Code. We are here to help you manage your new assets confidently and plan for a secure future.

Legal Considerations

  • •Compulsory Heirs (Forced Heirship): The law protects certain heirs by reserving a portion called the "legitime." This cannot be taken away, even by a will.
  • •Wills: A will allows distribution of the "free portion" according to your wishes.
  • •Equal Rights: The Constitution and Civil Code grant men and women equal inheritance rights.

Tax Implications

  • •Estate Tax: A flat 6% on the value of the net estate (total assets minus allowable deductions).
  • •Deductions: Funeral expenses, claims against the estate, and a standard deduction for the family home.
  • •Filing Deadline: The estate tax return must be filed within one year from the date of death.

Investment & Planning

  • •Financial Goal Setting: Define what you want to achieve: retirement, education, business, or legacy.
  • •PSE & Funds: The Philippine Stock Exchange, mutual funds, and UITFs offer diversified options.
  • •Real Estate: A robust market with strong investment potential.

A Clear Path for Your Inheritance in Malaysia

Malaysia's dual legal system and favorable tax environment offer a unique landscape for managing inherited wealth. Whether governed by civil or Sharia law, our advisors provide clarity and direction.

Legal Considerations

  • •Non-Muslims: The Distribution Act 1958 sets clear succession order (spouse gets 1/3, children share 2/3). A will provides freedom to distribute as you wish.
  • •Muslims: Faraid (Islamic Law) specifies fixed shares. A will can only bequeath up to 1/3 to non-heirs.
  • •Women's Rights: Under civil law, women have equal inheritance rights. Under Islamic law, shares are pre-determined by religious texts.

Tax Implications

  • •No Inheritance Tax: Abolished in 1991. You will not be taxed on the value of assets received.
  • •Real Property Gains Tax (RPGT): You may be liable if you sell inherited property, though exemptions exist including a one-time private residence exemption.

Investment & Planning

  • •ASNB Unit Trusts: Popular unit trust funds that are a cornerstone of many Malaysians' portfolios.
  • •Bursa Malaysia: Diversified stock portfolio for long-term growth.
  • •Trusts & Foundations: Sophisticated wealth management to ensure your legacy is managed to your long-term vision.

Secure Your Legacy in Thailand

Managing an inheritance in Thailand requires understanding the Civil and Commercial Code and newer inheritance tax laws. Our advisors help you secure assets, minimize your tax burden, and invest wisely.

Legal Considerations

  • •Statutory Heirs: Six classes of statutory heirs inherit in strict order of priority. Spouse and children are highest priority.
  • •Power of a Will: Highly recommended to ensure assets are distributed to your exact wishes.
  • •Equal Rights: Men and women have equal rights to inherit property under Thai law.

Tax Implications

  • •Inheritance Tax: Applies to inheritances over 100 million THB. 5% for descendants/ascendants, 10% for others.
  • •Property Transfer Fees: Registration and transfer fees at the Land Department, significantly lower for inheritance than direct sale.

Investment & Planning

  • •Financial Planning: Create a clear plan based on your life goals and risk tolerance.
  • •Thai Markets: The Stock Exchange of Thailand (SET), bonds, and a vibrant property market.
  • •Foreign Ownership: Foreigners generally cannot own land, but can own buildings or condo units.

Navigating Your Inheritance in Vietnam

Inheriting assets in Vietnam means engaging with its civil law system and specific tax regulations. As a woman in one of Asia's fastest-growing economies, understanding your rights and opportunities is paramount.

Legal Considerations

  • •Heirs-at-Law: First line includes spouse, parents, and children, who all inherit equal shares.
  • •Wills & Forced Heirs: A will gives control over distribution, but a portion is reserved for minor children, parents, and spouse.
  • •Equal Rights: Vietnamese law guarantees equal inheritance rights for men and women.

Tax Implications

  • •Inheritance Tax: 10% on the value of inherited assets exceeding 10 million VND.
  • •Family Exemption: Inheritance between close family members (husband/wife, parents/children, siblings) is exempt.

Investment & Planning

  • •Growing Market: Exciting opportunities in manufacturing, technology, and consumer goods with high growth potential.
  • •Real Estate: Popular investment, but foreigners should seek immediate legal advice on land ownership restrictions.
  • •Diversified Portfolio: We help align investments with Vietnam's growth story and your personal financial goals.
